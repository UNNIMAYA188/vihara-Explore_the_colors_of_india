Festivals of India

Explore the vibrant and diverse festivals celebrated across India

Popular National Festivals

Diwali

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, celebrates the victory of light over darkness, good over evil. It is celebrated with fireworks, sweets, and prayers to Goddess Lakshmi.

Holi

Holi, the Festival of Colors, celebrates the arrival of spring. People play with colored powders, sing songs, and enjoy delicious sweets.

Eid

Eid is an important religious festival observed by Muslims. It marks the end of Ramadan and is celebrated with feasts, prayers, and the giving of charity.

Main Festivals by State

Maharashtra

Ganesh Chaturthi: A popular festival dedicated to Lord Ganesha, celebrated with grand processions, prayers, and feasts.

West Bengal

Durga Puja: A major festival in honor of Goddess Durga, with elaborate decorations, processions, and rituals.

Tamil Nadu

Pongal: A harvest festival that involves feasting, family gatherings, and honoring the Sun God.

Uttar Pradesh

Mathura & Vrindavan Holi: A special celebration of Holi in the birthplace of Lord Krishna, known for its unique customs and rituals.

Kerala

Onam: A harvest festival celebrated with traditional dance, feasts, and a boat race. The festival honors the legendary King Mahabali and marks the homecoming of his spirit.

Rajasthan

Pushkar Camel Fair: A cultural and livestock fair held annually in Pushkar, with camel races, folk music, and vibrant markets.

Gujarat

Navratri: A nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga, celebrated with traditional dances like Garba and Dandiya.

Punjab

Lohri: A festival celebrating the harvest season with bonfires, folk songs, and dances like Bhangra.
